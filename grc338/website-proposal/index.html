Introduction

Posh Pottery

Posh Pottery is a fictional business located in Morro Bay, with its store right on the water. It operates as a small shop that offers classes and event bookings such as birthday parties. We also sell pottery out of the store. Customizable pottery sets can also be commissioned; Just give us a call or come on in!

Target audience

Our target audience encompasses anyone looking to learn a new skill, have a fun experience making pottery, or looking to buy items made by our in-house potter. We offer classes for those looking to get some hands-on experience.

The primary tasks and goals of people using this site is to gain more information or purchase something. The people that will use our website are those looking to purchase pottery, book classes or events, or learn more about the shop and where we are located. The contact information of the shop will also be available, giving site visitors ways to contact the shop, through email and phone.

About Us

Posh Pottery out of Morro Bay is anything but "posh". We like to get dirty and work with our hands to make some of the most rugged and refined pieces of pottery you will ever find! There is nothing quite like making something that came from your hands. Founders Debbie and Claire put their hobbies into a business over 8 years ago, and Posh Pottery came to be.

Classes and Events

Classes

Our class schedule for the month is provided upon request and is posted at the Morro Bay events center downtown. Because we are a small studio, we can't accomodate too many classes, but we will have them about 8 times a month. Please contact us via email or phone to learn more about times and skills being taught.

Events

Posh Pottery has hosted every type of event from birthday parties to bachelorette parties! We can accommodate up to 10 guests per party, and we can set up a fun activity to do. Posh Pottery does ask that your provide your own snacks and beverages, but to be mindful when consuming them because some of the materials that we work with in the pottery can be harmful to ingest!
